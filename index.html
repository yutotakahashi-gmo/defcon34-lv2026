Equipment list to lock by early July. Scenario knowledge below ↓

🎯 S1 — Eavesdropping (downlink interception)

Attack: An outsider captures the satellite's downlink and pulls out a hidden "classified image" — listening in on signals flying overhead.

Why it works: Radio is broadcast openly; anyone on the right frequency, pointing the right way, can receive it.

Visitor flow: frequency scan → point antenna via TLE → assemble the GNU Radio block puzzle → hidden image appears (pre-recorded IQ file).

Takeaway: reception needs three things aligned — right frequency, right antenna direction, right demodulation.

🎯 S2 — Uplink attack (spoofed command injection)

Attack: the attacker injects a fake uplink command and takes control of the satellite (e.g. "rotate the panel").

Why it works: older satellites often lack authentication — mimic the command format (opcode) and it executes as if real.

Physical payoff: the toy satellite's solar panel — tracking the light "sun" — suddenly spins out of control. Losing sun-tracking = cutting the satellite's power.

Takeaway: a legitimate command becomes an attack when its timing is weaponized; unauthenticated command structures are dangerous.

🎯 S3 — Telemetry spoofing (drone-mounted SDR)

Attack: the satellite is actually failing, but a fake "all systems normal" telemetry is sent to the ground, hiding the anomaly.

Why a drone: to inject into the ground antenna, transmit from the satellite's direction — a HackRF (playing the drone) floods fake signals.

Highlight: two screens contradict — sat sim says "ANOMALY IN PROGRESS", mission-control dashboard reads "All Systems Normal."

Takeaway: without authenticated telemetry, operators trust a fake status and can't see the real failure. Telemetry needs auth + encryption too.

🎯 S4 — Constellation chaos (orbital manipulation)

Attack: a forged orbit-maneuver command alters the satellite's path, causing a physical collision with another satellite.

Why it works: changing speed shifts the orbital elements (altitude, inclination, RAAN, ν) and redirects the satellite onto a collision course.

Highlight: a guaranteed Three.js collision animation on every run.

Takeaway: one compromised satellite cascades across the fleet (a miniature Kessler syndrome).

🪜 The story across all four

Escalating severity — same root cause throughout: signals are open to anyone, yet authentication and encryption are weak or absent.

1Listen — interception (steal information)
2Control — spoofed command (take over the satellite)
3Deceive — status falsification (hide the anomaly)
4Destroy — orbital manipulation (physical, fleet-wide)

📚 基礎知識 · Fundamentals

From zero — no space or hacking background needed. The groundwork that makes every scenario click.

🛰 How satellites talk to the ground

Satellites are in space, so no wires — they communicate by radio waves (RF), like your phone or Wi-Fi. Two directions:

↓Downlink — satellite → ground (status, photos)
↑Uplink — ground → satellite (commands: "turn", "take a photo")
📡Ground Station (GS) — the dish-antenna facility that sends/receives

⚠ Why every attack works: radio is open (anyone with a receiver can pick it up) + many older satellites have no encryption or authentication. "Open access" + "weak security" = every scenario in this exhibit.

🔑 Key terms in every scenario

TLE (Two-Line Element)2-line text of a satellite's orbit/position. Know the TLE → calculate exactly where to point the antenna.

Antenna az / elazimuth = horizontal bearing (N/S/E/W), elevation = angle above horizon (90° = overhead). Miss either → lose the signal.

RF parametersfrequency (which channel), bandwidth (how wide), sample rate / gain (how finely / loudly to record). Wrong frequency → only static.

HackRFpalm-sized SDR that can both receive and transmit. Here: intercepts signals and broadcasts spoofed ones.

IQ filea raw numerical "recording" of a radio wave — replay and analyze later to extract images or commands.

GNU Radiosoftware where you wire processing blocks (input → filter → demodulate → image). Less coding, more wiring puzzle.

Demodulationthe "radio wave → original data" conversion (back to 0s/1s, then images or commands).

OpenVSA / VSAthe team's virtual ground-station antenna software — tracks satellites by TLE and controls TX/RX. The main visitor interface.

Telemetry (TM)the satellite's continuous "status report" (battery, temp, attitude, panel angle). → key in S3.

Command (C2, opcode)ground → satellite instructions in a fixed format (a number mapped to an action = opcode). → forged in S2–S4.

Constellationmany satellites run as one coordinated fleet (Starlink, GPS). → the theme of S4.

🎯 The attacks, precisely

interceptionpassively capturing downlink RF to read data — 盗聴

jammingoverpowering the link to deny service — 電波妨害

spoofingtransmitting forged signals a receiver trusts — なりすまし送信

replayre-sending a valid captured command — 正規コマンドの再送

command injectioncrafting a valid-format command to control the bus — バス制御の奪取

telemetry falsificationfeeding operators fake health data — 偽テレメトリで異常を隠す

orbital manipulationforging maneuver commands to move or collide — 軌道操作・衝突

🔐 Defenses that matter

authenticationverify the command actually came from the operator — 送信元の検証

encryptionprotect confidentiality of TT&C and payload data — 暗号化

anti-replaynonces / timestamps so old commands can't be reused — 再送防止

link security (CCSDS SDLS)auth + encryption at the space data-link layer — リンク層保護

zero trustnever trust unauthenticated telemetry or commands — 無検証データを信じない

segmentationisolate and harden the ground network — 地上系の分離・堅牢化
